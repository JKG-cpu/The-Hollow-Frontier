A pixel art shooter game where you collect bounties from monsters. You start in a town with basic gear. You go around to NPCs to accept quests, which is when they tell you about all the horrors that have been going around town.

After each quest is accepted, you get a clue to where that monster resides, you can travel around the open world. In order to complete the quest, you must kill whatever you have been asked to kill, bring the head back, and then you get the rewards.

You can accept multiple quests, which will all be in your quest book (where the clues and information are).

The game
-  Is a 2D side scroller
-  Will have different houses / shops you can enter
-  Is a solo game (no multi player)
-  A ambient horror game

## Fears
Fears to focus on for the *ambiance*

1. Fear of the unknown
	1. Don't immediately show the monster
	2. Leave things like
		- Scratch marks
		- An animal dead with no visible wounds
		- Empty camps with food still cooking
		- Broken wagons
		- Blood trails that suddenly stop
		- Strange footprints that disappear
		- Trees bent or broken unnaturally
		- Missing posters nailed to trees
		- Half-finished journal entries
		- Fresh campfires with nobody nearby

2. Fear of being hunted
	1. Like you hear footsteps behind you, you stop, they stop, etc.
	2. Something follows you just outside your vision
	3. Eyes appear in the distance, then disappear
	4. The monster circles around you instead of attacking
	5. You occasionally hear breathing behind you
	6. Tracks begin following your own

3. Isolation
	1. Slow, quiet music
	2. Few NPCs around
	3. Wind blowing, then stopping
	4. Large empty areas between towns
	5. Long periods with no music
	6. Wildlife suddenly disappears
	7. No signs of civilization for miles

4. Fear of darkness
	1. Night reduces visibility ***a lot***
		- View flashes from lightning
		- Very little moonlight
		- Lantern becomes your main light source
		- Shadows hide movement
		- Some monsters only appear at night

5. Fear of something watching
	1. Eyes on cliffs
	2. Animals staring as you pass by
	3. Figures standing on rooftops
	4. Silhouettes between trees
	5. Windows with someone watching inside
	6. Crows watching from fences
	7. Things disappear when approached

6. Fear of sound
	1. Animals
	2. Children laughing
	3. Chains dragging
	4. Wood creaking
	5. Heavy breathing
	6. Whispering
	7. Distant screams
	8. Horse footsteps when no horse is nearby
	9. Knocking inside abandoned buildings
	10. Complete silence before danger

7. Fear of the woods
	1. Fog
	2. A lot of paths
	3. Random signs
	4. Trees all look the same
	5. Strange carvings in trees
	6. Broken fences leading nowhere
	7. Hanging lanterns with no owners
	8. Trails that seem to loop back
	9. Dead animals hanging from branches

8. Fear of abandoned places
	1. Empty towns
	2. Houses with doors left open
	3. Dinner still sitting on tables
	4. Lights still burning
	5. Rocking chairs moving
	6. Mines with distant pickaxe sounds
	7. Churches with no congregation

9. Fear of things that aren't quite right
	1. Deer standing on two legs
	2. Horses with glowing eyes
	3. Birds that don't fly away
	4. NPCs acting strangely
	5. Shadows moving on their own
	6. Objects changing position when you look away

10. Fear of silence
	1. Music suddenly stops
	2. Wind disappears
	3. No insects or birds
	4. Everything becomes completely quiet
	5. Silence before a monster encounter

11. Fear of getting lost
	1. Trails split often
	2. Fog hides landmarks
	3. Maps don't show everything
	4. Caves branch into multiple paths
	5. Landmarks seem different on the way back

12. Fear of massive creatures
	1. Huge footprints
	2. Trees knocked over
	3. The ground shakes
	4. Roars echo across the map
	5. You occasionally see a giant silhouette far away

13. Fear of limited resources
	1. Low ammunition
	2. Limited healing
	3. Lantern oil can run out
	4. Shops are far apart
	5. Every shot matters

14. Fear of unnatural weather
	1. Thick fog rolls in
	2. Blood-red moon
	3. Green lightning
	4. Sudden storms
	5. Ash falling from the sky
	6. Wind blowing the wrong direction

15. Fear of distorted humanity
	1. Monsters that were once people
	2. Twisted faces
	3. Unnaturally long limbs
	4. Human voices coming from monsters
	5. Former bounty hunters who became monsters

Monster Hunt Structure
1. Accept bounty
2. Talk to witnesses
3. Search the area
4. Find clues
5. Hear strange sounds
6. Catch brief glimpses of the monster
7. Follow tracks to its territory
8. Explore its lair
9. Fight the monster
10. Collect its head as proof
11. Return to town for your reward

Don't show any health bars, the way a player tells when a monster is almost dead, is by the way it acts. Like limping, missing limbs, etc.

### Combat system
- Every fight should feel like hunting a dangerous predator, not clearing enemies.
- Monsters should be deadly, but not bullet sponges.
- Every bullet should matter.
- The tension should begin before the fight starts.

Player Combat
- Revolver
	- High damage
	- 6 shots
	- Slow reload
	- Accurate
- Lever Action Rifle
	- Long range
	- High damage
	- Slow fire rate
- Shotgun
	- Massive close-range damage
	- Very short range
- Repeater
	- Faster fire rate
	- Lower damage
- Hunting Knife
	- Fast melee attack
	- Last resort
- Hatchet
	- Slower melee
	- High damage

### Movement
- Sprint
- Dodge Roll
	- Short invincibility frames
	- Uses stamina
- Stamina
	- Used for sprinting
	- Used for dodge rolling
	- Used for heavy melee attacks
	- Does NOT affect shooting

### Combat Loop
1. Accept bounty
2. Gather information
3. Travel to the location
4. Search for clues
5. Follow tracks
6. Hear strange sounds
7. Catch brief glimpses of the monster
8. Monster notices you
9. Hunt begins
10. Monster retreats to its den
11. Final fight
12. Collect its head
13. Return to town

### Monster AI
- Monsters should behave like predators instead of running directly at the player.
- They should
	- Circle the player
	- Hide behind terrain
	- Retreat after attacking
	- Ambush from off-screen
	- Fake attacks to bait dodges
	- Wait for reloads before attacking
	- Become more aggressive when wounded

### Monster Weak Points
- Eyes
- Head
- Limbs
- Horns
- Glowing body parts
- Armor plates

Damaging weak points can
- Slow movement
- Remove attacks
- Stagger the monster
- Expose new weak points

### Boss Phases
Instead of using a health bar, show damage through behavior.

##### Healthy
- Calm
- Confident
- Uses basic attacks

##### Injured
- Begins limping
- Breathes heavier
- Attacks become more aggressive

##### Critical
- Desperate
- Faster attacks
- New attacks
- Attempts to flee
- Makes mistakes

##### Environment During Fights
- Dense forests
- Abandoned mines
- Caves
- Churches
- Swamps
- Cliffs
- Farms
- Cemeteries

##### Environmental Hazards
- Falling rocks
- Collapsing floors
- Spike pits
- Explosive barrels
- Deep water
- Fire
- Poison plants

##### Preparation Before Hunts
- Buy ammunition
- Buy healing supplies
- Bring lantern oil
- Bring traps
- Bring throwable items

##### Difficulty Idea
- Avoid increasing monster health just to make fights harder.
- Increase difficulty through
	- Smarter AI
	- Faster reactions
	- New attacks
	- Better ambushes
	- More dangerous environments
	- Less predictable behavior

##### Things **NOT** to Do
- Huge health bars
- Endless enemy waves
- Bullet sponge bosses
- Constant combat
- Bosses that only walk toward the player

### Goal
Every hunt should feel unique, tense, and dangerous. The player should feel like they survived a terrifying encounter rather than simply defeating another enemy.
